STUDENTS ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

  • 1.Educational Qualifications:Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related fields is essential for most software companies.
  • 2.Technical Skills:Strong knowledge of programming languages, data structures, and algorithms is highly valued.
  • 3.Problem-Solving and Analytical Abilities:Critical thinking and logical problem-solving skills are crucial in software development.
  • 4.Communication Skills:Effective communication is essential for teamwork and client interactions
  • 5.Academic Performance:Good academic scores (above 60%) are generally expected, but companies may be flexible based on other skills.
  • 6.Internships and Projects:Relevant internships and personal projects demonstrate practical experience.
  • 7.Adaptability and Learning Ability:Willingness to learn and adapt to a fast-paced work environment is appreciated.
  • 8.Certification:Certifications in relevant technologies can enhance your profile but are not always mandatory.
  • 9.Pass-out Year:Some companies may prefer recent graduates, but it's not a strict criterion for all.
